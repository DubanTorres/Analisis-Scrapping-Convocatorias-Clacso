GUIDELINES 
 2013 GEMINI-CONICYT POSTDOCTORAL POSITION COMPETITION 
NATIONAL PROGRAM 
For the Development of Astronomy and Related Sciences 
 
 
1. 
GENERAL BACKGROUND 
 
This competition is developed within the general framework of the “Scientific 
Collaboration Agreement to Promote Astronomy Research”, dated September 1, 2004, 
and its subsequent modifications, signed by the National Commission for Scientific and 
Technological Research (CONICYT) and the GEMINI Astronomy Consortium, an 
international cooperation project with participation of the United States, the United 
Kingdom, Canada, Chile, Australia, Argentina and Brazil.  
 
CONICYT is the beneficiary and administrator of the Fund for the National Development 
of Chilean Astronomy and Related Sciences. 
 
2. 
COMPETITION BACKGROUND  
 
In order to support the operation of the GEMINI telescopes, each of the Consortium 
member countries has a National Office dedicated to GEMINI matters. 
 
The GEMINI-CONICYT Office in Chile is responsible for supporting astronomers in 
Chilean institutions, both before they carry out observations through the GEMINI-
South telescope as well as after they have obtained their data. This support is aimed at 
maximizing the intellectual return, on behalf of the Chilean scientific community, 
resulting in scientific opportunities provided by the GEMINI Observatory. 
 
To that end, an Astronomy or Astrophysics doctorate will be hired to distribute his/her 
work time between the CONICYT GEMINI Office and the development of independent 
research in Optical or Infrared Astronomy. The latter must be carried out in one of the 
Chilean universities that develop research in Astronomy. 
 
This has been named as the GEMINI-CONICYT Postdoctoral Position. 
 
2.1 
Competition Objectives 
 
The objective of the GEMINI-CONICYT Postdoctoral Position is to maintain the 
support provided to the Chilean astronomical community in order for it to make 
the most effective possible use of the GEMINI-South Observatory’s facilities. It 
is expected that this support will eventually reflect in the number of publications 
based on observations carried out by the Chilean astronomical community 
through this telescope. 
 
2.2. 
Applicants 
 
This competition is open to Chilean or foreign researchers who have obtained 
their Astronomy or Astrophysics doctorate degree after December 31, 2006 or 
  
2 
who are in the process of obtaining it 30 days prior to the date on which the 
position will initiate operations.  
 
The applicant must have experience in observations with optical or infrared 
instruments, including the reduction and analysis of data obtained with it. 
 
He/she must have, at the time of application, the sponsorship of a national 
university that will harbor him/her. It is desirable that the research planned for the 
duration of the position be carried out in collaboration with researchers from that 
institution. 
 
Fluency in spoken and written English is a requirement. 
 
2.3. 
Tasks to be Carried Out by the Beneficiary 
 
The work to be carried out by the beneficiary is related to all of the tasks of the 
GEMINI-CONICYT Office. The selected applicant will need to dedicate 60% of 
his/her day to the activities of the GEMINI Office. 
 
His/her responsibilities at the GEMINI-CONICYT Office, where he/she will work 
closely with the astronomer in charge of the Office, will be: 
 
a) To assist users in the preparation of observation proposals (Phase I), 
preparation of observations (Phase II) and data reduction. 
 
b) To carry out periodical work stays at the GEMINI-South Observatory in order 
to be up-to-date with the instruments and the general work of the 
Observatory. 
 
c) To deliver technical presentations in the Astronomy Departments of Chilean 
Universities. 
 
d) Apply for GEMINI-South telescope time. The proposed research must focus on 
tasks in which GEMINI has a greater potential scientific impact. 
 
e) To contribute to scientific activity in the University harboring him/her. 
 
2.4. 
Duration of the Postdoctoral Position 
 
The postdoctoral position will have a four-year duration, starting on January 1, 
2013 regardless of the actual date of completion of the administrative act 
approving the corresponding agreement between CONICYT and the beneficiary, 
which should occur during the first semester of 2013. 
 
Nevertheless, the current position may be extended for one more year if there 
is a prior formal authorization by CONICYT. 
 
2.5. 
Funding 
 
2.5.1. An annual amount of CLP $17,000,000.-(seventeen million pesos) 
for living expenses is included, which will be transferred to the 
postdoctoral researcher in 12 monthly installments. 
  
3 
 
2.5.2. In addition, the applicant may request up to CLP $3,000,000.- (three 
million pesos) to cover travel expenses (tickets and per diems) directly 
related to the proposed research studies or the presentation of their 
results and for operation expenses (laboratory supplies, books, journals 
and subscriptions, registration in congresses, hiring services, minor 
expenses and others). 
 
2.5.3. The above does not include travel expenses derived from his/her tasks at 
the GEMINI-CONICYT Office, which will be covered by the Office. 
 
2.5.4. An annual health insurance Premium for the beneficiary will be covered for 
a maximum amount of CLP $400,000.- (four hundred thousand 
pesos) per year. 
 
This amount will be adjusted annually in accordance to the Consumer Price Index 
(IPC by its acronym in Spanish) of the previous year.

Eligibility  
 
Only applications meeting the requirements and conditions set forth in these 
competition guidelines will go on to the evaluation process. Applications that do 
not fulfill the conditions of these guidelines will be declared inadmissible and, 
therefore, will not go on to the evaluation phase. This decision will be formally 
communicated by CONICYT to the applicant. 
 
The following are the eligibility criteria for this competition:

4. 
PROJECT EVALUATION AND AWARDING PROCESS 
 
4.1. 
Evaluation and Selection 
 
Applications will be evaluated by the GEMINI-CONICYT Fund Council, appointed 
by Exempt Resolution N°3560, dated August 10, 2012.  
 
4.2. 
Evaluation Criteria and Weights 
 
The following factors will be considered in the evaluation:  
 
Criteria 
Weight 
1. Academic background of the applicant 
50% 
2. Academic quality of the research proposal 
35% 
3. Quality of the academic unit in which the research project

15% 
 
Each evaluation criteria may be assigned a score from 0 to 5. It is allowed to 
use half points but not lesser fractions. In general, the approval threshold is 
estimated at three points. However, the evaluation Committee may deem 
necessary to raise that threshold depending on the applications submitted.  
 
Score definition: 
 
 
0. Does not 
qualify 
The proposal does not adequately comply with/approach the 
criterion analyzed or cannot be evaluated due to the lack of 
background information or to incomplete information. 
1. Deficient 
The proposal does not adequately comply with/approach the 
aspects of the criterion or presents serious inherent 
deficiencies. 
2. Fair 
The proposal does comply with/approach in general terms the 
aspects of the criterion, but there are important deficiencies. 
3. Good 
The proposal correctly complies with/approaches the aspects 
of the criterion, although it requires certain improvements. 
4. Very Good 
The proposal does comply with/approach the aspects of the 
criterion in a very good way, although some improvements 
can be made. 
  
5 
5. Excellent 
The proposal does comply with/approach the aspects relevant 
to the criterion in an outstanding manner. Any weakness is 
minor. 
 
Favorable consideration will be given to applicants who have publications based 
on observations made through GEMINI telescopes. 
 
4.3. 
Awarding of Projects 
 
CONICYT, through the corresponding administrative act, will approve the ruling 
and will award the competition, based on the recommendations made by the 
GEMINI-CONICYT Fund Council.  
 
The ruling may include a waiting list, if deemed necessary by the GEMINI-
CONICYT Fund Council. This list will be used whenever a beneficiary forfeits his 
or her right to the funds or is unable to use them. This waiting list will apply, in 
accordance to the order established by the GEMINI-CONICYT Fund Council. 
 
Under equal conditions, preference will be given to Chilean nationals. 
 
Should any applicant wish to contest the award ruling, he/she may file an 
appeal for reversal to the Executive Director of CONICYT within 5 working days 
since the notice was issued. 
 
4.4. 
Competition Ruling 
 
Once the final ruling has been made, the results will be posted on CONICYT’s 
website, where the waiting list will also be included if it exists. The competition 
results will also be notified via certified mail to all applicants.  
 
If the waiting list is used, the beneficiary(ies) will be notified via certified mail. 
 
After the awarding process, CONICYT will communicate beneficiaries the date 
on which they must sign the agreement with CONICYT, requesting the 
necessary documents for that purpose. The agreement will expressly set forth 
the rights and obligations of each party and the project duration as well as all 
clauses that better safeguard the compliance with the objectives of this 
competition.

6. PROJECT MONITORING 
 
6.1. 
Submission of Reports 
 
By the end of the first year of execution of the agreement, a performance 
evaluation will be done. This must be approved by the GEMINI-CONICYT Fund 
Council, taking into consideration the technical project progress report.  
 
 
6.2. 
Statements of Expenses 
 
The beneficiary must submit the corresponding statement of expenses for any 
travel expenses related to the research and for operational expenses, both of 
them described in section 2.5.2 of these guidelines, which may not exceed the 
maximum amount of CLP $3,000,000.- (three million pesos). CONICYT is 
responsible for the Control and Supervision of transferred resources. Therefore, 
the beneficiary must comply with the following requirements: 
 
 
The original documents that support the statement of expenses must be 
submitted to CONICYT for review and approval before any new resource 
transfers are made. The statement of expenses will be returned to the 
beneficiary upon revision. In the event that a document was reported 
and/or declared in more than one CONICYT program, this institution 
reserves the right to sanction the early termination, ipso facto, of the 
projects involved and request the full or partial restitution of the 
transferred resources. If the transferred resources are used to any other 
ends different to those indicated in the project, they must be reinstated to 
CONICYT. 
 
 
The funds transferred to the beneficiaries will be governed, as applicable, 
by Resolution N° 759/2003 Office of the Comptroller General of the 
Republic. 
 
 
It is forbidden for beneficiaries to invest the funds transferred by CONICYT 
in any type of financial instruments, be it of fixed or variable return and 
short or long-term (mutual funds, stocks, fixed-term deposits, bonds, 
etc.) with or without financial risk. 
 
 
The beneficiary will have a deadline of one year to submit a statement of 
expenses of the transferred resources.
