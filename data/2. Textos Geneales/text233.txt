1. ANTECEDENTES GENERALES 
 
En el año 2006, CONICYT crea el Programa de Astronomía, con la misión de apoyar y fomentar el 
desarrollo de la astronomía nacional, posicionar esta disciplina como un área estratégica de 
desarrollo de la ciencia en nuestro país y lograr convertir a Chile en una potencia mundial.  
Líneas de Acción: 
 
Apoyo, fortalecimiento y divulgación de la Astronomía para relevar esta disciplina como 
modelo estratégico de desarrollo de la ciencia en el país y lograr que Chile sea una potencia 
mundial en esta área. 
 
Promoción de la cooperación científica, tecnológica y atracción de nuevos proyectos e 
inversiones en astronomía, a través de alianzas estratégicas nacionales e internacionales. 
 
1.1 Llamado a Concurso: 
 
El presente Programa se desarrolla dentro del marco general del “Memorando de Entendimiento de 
Cooperación Científica para el Desarrollo de Investigación Astronómica", aprobado bajo Resolución 
Afecta N°114 del 28 de noviembre del 2013, suscrito entre la Comisión Nacional de Investigación 
Científica y Tecnológica (CONICYT) y la Academia China de Ciencias (CAS), el cual se establece un 
programa de colaboración  a través del Fondo CHINA-CONICYT para el desarrollo de la investigación 
astronómica conjunta, en lo sucesivo el “Fondo”. Las actividades establecidas por el Memorando de 
Entendimiento comprenden colaboraciones bilaterales en investigación astronómica, incluyendo el 
apoyo a jóvenes astrónomos/as a través de becas de postdoctorado, entre otros. 
 
El Fondo es administrado por CONICYT, y la adjudicación estará a cargo del Comité Conjunto CAS-
CONICYT, de acuerdo a lo estipulado en el Memorando de Entendimiento firmado por ambos países. 
 
El presente llamado a concursos financiará propuestas de investigación de Posiciones Postdoctorales, 
por medio del Fondo CHINA-CONICYT en su 2da Convocatoria.

1.2  Objetivo del concurso: 
 
El objetivo principal de este concurso es fortalecer la cooperación entre Chile y China en Astronomía, 
a través de la inserción de jóvenes investigadores/as con grado de Doctor, mediante la realización de 
propuestas de investigación en instituciones chilenas, con la posibilidad de continuar en China. De 
esta manera contribuir al desarrollo científico, fortalecido por la participación de ambos países.

A cada criterio de evaluación se le debe asignar una puntuación de 0 a 5, pudiéndose utilizar medios 
puntos pero no fracciones menores, estimándose en general, que el umbral de aprobación no podrá 
ser inferior de tres puntos. 
 
Definición de los puntajes:  
0. No califica 
La propuesta no cumple/aborda el criterio bajo análisis o no puede ser 
evaluada debido a la falta de antecedentes o información incompleta. 
1. Deficiente 
La propuesta no cumple/aborda adecuadamente los aspectos del 
criterio o hay graves deficiencias inherentes. 
2. Regular 
La propuesta cumple/aborda en términos generales los aspectos del 
criterio, pero existen importantes deficiencias. 
3. Bueno 
La propuesta cumple/aborda correctamente los aspectos del criterio, 
aunque requiere ciertas mejoras. 
4. Muy bueno 
La propuesta cumple/aborda los aspectos del criterio de muy buena 
manera, aun cuando son posibles ciertas mejoras.  
5. Excelente 
La propuesta cumple/aborda de manera sobresaliente todos los 
aspectos relevantes del criterio en cuestión. Cualquier debilidad es muy 
menor.

5. FALLO Y CONDICIONES DE ADJUDICACIÓN 
 
5.1 Mecanismo de selección:  
 
En la Propuesta de Fallo a CONICYT, se enlistan los investigadores seleccionados, en orden 
descendente (de mayor a menor puntuación), definiéndose en general, que el umbral de aprobación 
no podrá ser inferior de 3 puntos. 
 
5.2 Adjudicación de las Propuestas: 
 
CONICYT, a través del acto administrativo correspondiente, aprobará el fallo y adjudicará el concurso, 
sobre la base de la propuesta realizada por el Comité Conjunto CAS-CONICYT. El acto administrativo 
que adjudique el presente concurso será notificado mediante correo electrónico determinado 
expresamente por los postulantes para estos efectos, a todos los postulantes, aunque no hayan salido 
favorecidos, además de ser publicados en la página web de CONICYT. 
Respecto de la resolución adjudicataria, los postulantes podrán interponer el recurso de reposición 
correspondiente, ante el Jefe Superior del Servicio, en el plazo de 5 días hábiles desde la notificación.

7. TRANSFERENCIA DE RECURSOS 
 
Los recursos asignados a cada Posición Postdoctoral,  serán  transferidos en 1 o más cuotas por año,

de instrumentos, financiero de renta fija o variable de corto o largo plazo (fondos mutuos, acciones,

a los fines recién mencionados, CONICYT podrá exigir el reintegro total o parcial de los recursos ya 
entregados.

reintegrados a CONICYT.

1. GENERAL INFORMATION 
 
In 2006, CONICYT created the Astronomy Program with the aim of supporting and fostering Chilean 
Astronomy, as well as to position this discipline as a strategic area for the development of Science in 
Chile, and turn the country into a key player worldwide.  
Lines of action: 
 
Support, strengthen and disseminate Astronomy to position this discipline as a strategic 
model for the development of Science in Chile, in order to turn the country into a key player 
worldwide in this field. 
 
Promote scientific and technological cooperation, as well as attract new projects and 
investment in Astronomy, through both domestic and international strategic partnerships. 
 
1.1 Call for proposals: 
 
This program is developed within the framework of the "Memorandum of Understanding on Scientific 
Cooperation for the Development of Astronomical Research", approved under Regulation No 114 
from November 28th, 2013. It was signed by the National Commission for Scientific and Technological 
Research (CONICYT) and the Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS). It creates a collaborative program 
through the CHINA-CONICYT Fund, hereinafter the "Fund", aimed at conducting joint astronomical 
research. The activities set out by the Memorandum of Understanding include bilateral collaboration 
in astronomical research, including supporting young astronomers through postdoctoral fellowships, 
among others.

1.2 Objective of the call: 
 
The main objective of this call is to strengthen cooperation between Chile and China in Astronomy 
through the inclusion of young researchers with doctorate degrees, by engaging in research 
proposals in Chilean institutions, with the possibility of continuing them in China. Thus, they will 
contribute to scientific development, strengthened by the involvement of both countries.

3. ADMISSIBILITY 
 
3.1 Admissibility process: 
 
The members of the Astronomy Program review the documentation for each proposal and prepare 
a list of admissible valid submissions, according to the fulfillment of the requirements and documents 
stated on the rules. Applicants who do not comply with the rules of this call, its conditions and 
requirements, or who do not deliver all the requested information, will be excluded and will not enter 
the evaluation process. 
In the event of inadmissible proposals, postdoctoral researchers will be notified by e-mail.  
 
3.2 Documents and conditions: 
 
The proposals must be submitted in English, including all the documents listed below: 
 
Curriculum vitae. 
 
Copy of the doctorate degree or other certificate attesting to the Doctoral condition. 
 
Two (2) letters of recommendation.  
 
Letter of support from the host organization, signed by the legal representative of 
the institution that will host the applicant. This letter must specify the name of the 
research sponsor. 
 
Applicants may expressly authorize CONICYT to send all notifications for this call via e-mail, whether 
they be those related to the application phase, the signature of the agreement and for the entire

duration of the project, including the monitoring of compliance with obligations phase. For the 
purposes stated above, the applicant shall provide an e-mail address in their application, in order to

which the project will not get to the evaluation phase: 
1. At the time of the application and during the resolution of this call, both the postdoctoral

project may be grounds for it to be declared inadmissible, to rescind the award, or an early

reimbursement of the funds awarded, if applicable.

5. DECISION AND AWARDING CONDITIONS 
 
5.1 Selection mechanism:  
 
In the decision proposal given to CONICYT, the selected researchers are listed in descending order 
(from highest to lowest score), defining in general that the approval threshold may not be lower than 
three points. 
 
5.2 Awarding of proposals: 
 
CONICYT, through the corresponding administrative act, will approve the decision and award the call, 
based on the proposal made by the CAS-CONICYT Joint Committee. The administrative act awarding 
this call will be sent by e-mail to the addresses provided by the applicants for these purposes. All 
applicants will be notified, whether they have been favored or not, and it will also be published on 
CONICYT's website. 
As for the awarding decision, applicants may appeal to the Head of the Service within five business 
days from the notification. 
 
6. AGREEMENT 
 
6.1 Signing of the agreement: 
 
The financing agreements will be signed between CONICYT and the selected postdoctoral researcher 
for a period of one to two years, as appropriate. The financing agreement will expressly define the 
rights and obligations of each party, the duration and start date of the postdoctoral position, and all 
those clauses that best safeguard the fulfillment of the objectives of this call.  
 
The agreement will only be valid after the complete processing of the administrative act that 
approves it. 
 
When signing the agreement, the awarded must submit the following documents: 
 
a) Two (2) copies of the financing agreement. 
b) Letter of acceptance signed by the selected postdoctoral researcher.  
c) Special mandate and promissory note giving power to CONICYT, according to the format and 
the content provided by this service. Thus, in case of non-compliance with economic 
obligations, CONICYT may specifically fill the promissory note on behalf of the beneficiary, 
setting its expiration date and the due amounts. 
d) Copy of their identity card or passport, as appropriate: foreign researchers with awarded 
proposals must show proof of residence in the country through a permanent or temporary 
residence visa. For the purposes of processing the agreement, temporary visa holders must

7. TRANSFER OF FUNDS 
 
The funds allocated to each postdoctoral position will be transferred in one or more installments per 
year, according to what the parties define in the agreement, and shall be governed, as appropriate, 
by Regulation No 759/2003 of the Comptroller General of the Republic. 
 
Nevertheless, the continuity of the postdoctoral position and, consequently, the provision of funds 
for the following phases (year 2 or year 3, if applicable), depend on the approval of the technical 
progress reports and expense reports, and also on CONICYT's budgetary availability. 
 
Beneficiaries are forbidden from investing the funds transferred by CONICYT in any financial 
instruments such as fixed or variable income, short or long term (mutual funds, stocks, time deposits, 
bonds, etc.) with or without financial risk. If the funds transferred are allocated to the above-
mentioned purposes, CONICYT may demand the full or partial reimbursement of the funds already 
provided. 
 
If the transferred resources are used for purposes other than those specified in the project, they must 
be returned to CONICYT.

8. MONITORING AND CONTROL 
 
8.1 Technical follow-up: Progress and final reports: 
 
The postdoctoral researcher must send an annual scientific progress report to CONICYT, as 
appropriate, at the end of the postdoctoral position. 
 
8.2 Changes: 
 
During the execution of the project, the postdoctoral researcher may request in writing, in a justified 
and timely manner, that CONICYT, through its Astronomy Program, make modifications to the 
agreement. For example: changes in activities and/or members of the project, redistribution of 
benefits, project deadlines, among others. Such modifications shall become effective once authorized 
by CONICYT, after a favorable report from the Astronomy Program. 
 
 
9. EXPENSE REPORTS 
 
After each execution period of the project, the postdoctoral researcher must submit the expense 
report to CONICYT's Expense Control Unit of the Department of Administration and Finance 
(UCR/DAF), along with the original documentation supporting the expenditures. 
For expense report purposes, only those expenses issued to the postdoctoral researcher will be 
accepted, under no circumstances shall they be issued to CONICYT. 
CONICYT will request a checking account meant exclusively for the management of the funds given 
to projects receiving over 50 million pesos or 20 million, annually. In addition, the corresponding bank 
statements for all transactions must be submitted, considering such documents as mandatory and 
part of the expense report. 
All expenses related to the execution of the postdoctoral position must be declared (installation costs 
item, travel, health benefits and/or operating expenses), following the guidelines for expense 
reporting, according to the instructions contained in the aforementioned guidelines. 
Any kind of compensation will not be accepted as project expenses (for instance: in replacement of 
legal notice, legal vacations and years of service, all event, etc.), as they do not align with the 
objectives of projects funded by CONICYT. 
The expense reports will be returned to the postdoctoral researcher once they have been reviewed.

10. RECOGNITION 
 
The contribution from the fund must be recognized in every activity funded by it, such as publications 
and scientific reports (journal articles, theses, papers, etc.) and promotional materials according to 
the following suggested text (or its equivalent in another language): "This work was supported by 
CONICYT, the Astronomy Program and the second call of the CHINA-CONICYT Fund". In case of purchase 
of equipment, a plaque stating the above must be attached to the equipment.
